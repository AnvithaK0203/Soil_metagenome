# SMHI Limitations

- The SMHI is based on `24` samples only.
- The score is cohort-relative. Adding new samples can change the scaling and the ranking.
- The score has no external validation against pH, NPK, moisture, temperature, yield, crop outcome, or a reference soil-health assay.
- Richness and diversity are not universal proxies for agronomic health in every soil context.
- Unclassified proportion depends partly on the current reference database and classification behavior, not only on biology.
- The optional centroid-distance term measures typicality within this cohort, not “health” in a universal sense.
- The SMHI does not identify causal mechanisms.
- The SMHI must not be used as a crop recommendation score.
- The SMHI must not be presented as a final validated soil-health diagnosis.
